Order cards by: Team, Rule, Rows

One of the common reactions participants had is “but I’m not an artist”. Some of their frustrations in having to improvise around a set of rules in a restricted amount of time were expressed in the titles of the cards, for example: “WTF” (C5), “Close but no cigar” (C7), “I tried” (C9), “no time” (F3), and “these rules are confusing” (G6).

The constaint in time prevented the students from “over-thinking” as they had to tackle and compromise between the multiple rules. Card number 5 was the most challenging in that it had the highest number of adjacent cards (rules 2,4,5,6 & 8) which was said to be left for last because none of the members wanted to take on that challenge.

Due to the time constraint it is expected that some of the rules may be ignored entirely or accidently skipped in order to complete the 9 tiles on time. When grouping the cards by card number this becomes more evident, for example, the 3D sphere in group type number 4.

  • The Nine Rivals
  • Form groups of 1 or more players. Each group gets 9 blank cards.
  • Label each card with a unique number in the top left corner.
  • Each card is assigned a unique rule.
  • Each player is assigned one colored pen.
  1. Pick one card, note the assigned rule of your card.
  2. Draw something on your card that follows its own rule AND the rules of it’s adjacent cards. (see Figure 1).
  3. Add a short title to your drawing in the bottom left corner.
  4. Move on to another card until all 9 cards have drawings.
